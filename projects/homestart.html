Home-Start Northampton.

Home-start Northampton is a voluntary organisation committed to promoting the welfare of families with at least one child under five years of age and living in the Borough of Northampton.
At the beginning of 2017, I and three of my university colleagues were informed that the organisation intends to have a new, modern image that represents their values in a more appropriate way. We were convinced by their cause and worked as volunteers during that year on their new website.

Visit Website

01. The Team

The website was the result of a collaborative work between me and three university colleagues. As we already knew each other well, the project roles were established before we even started. However, as this was our first real project outside university, we thought that it would be beneficial, in terms of learning, if each of us will get involved in each process in a similar manner.

02. Design

The organisation main goal was to engage with its users closely, but also to attract new volunteers and spread the word about the help they can offer. We decided to build a WordPress website so that the staff can have control over the content and users, publish posts, create events and much more.
As the charity is being part of Home-Start UK, we had to closely respect the company brand colours and typography.

By studying similar websites, we concluded that the overall design needed to be friendly, colourful and engaging. To ensure that we’re targeting the right audience, we had several meetings with the Home-Start trustees and volunteers in which we built user profiles and UX scenarios.

03. Technology

As mentioned above, the website was developed entirely using WordPress. Each team member had to integrate various plugins and create a set of pages using the content provided. I also wanted to deliver a fast website so I implemented the W3 Total Cache and Autoptimize plugins. Later on, I explained the importance of SEO to the staff and how important is to keep the website up-to-date in terms of content, I implemented Yoast SEO and registered the website in the Google’s Search Console and Analytics applications.

04. The Result

In January 2018, after we presented the final version of the website to the Home-Start Northampton staff we had positive feedback and everyone was eager to learn to use WordPress so that they can keep the website updated and engaging. We implemented small extra features to the member's area, created a training document for the staff and at the end of February, we replaced the old website.
The results were better than we expected as the long-time Home-Start users were satisfied with the new design and more importantly, for having a fully responsive website which they can access anywhere on any device.
Currently, I’m keeping in touch with the staff and offer my support if anyone gets stuck of if new issues occur.

05. Summary

I believe that Home-Start was a great project for everyone involved. I personally enjoyed giving something back to the people that are offering to help the local community on a daily basis. Professionally, I learned a lot and was I pleased to work with my friends on a non-fictive project, with real “clients”.
